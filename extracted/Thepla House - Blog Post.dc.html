Why we'll never use maida

By TejalMay 20264 min read

Ask anyone what makes a roti feel light and fluffy at a restaurant, and the honest answer is often the same: maida. Refined white flour is cheap, soft and forgiving. It's also stripped of the bran and germ — the parts of the wheat that actually do you good.

At Thepla House, we made a decision early on that we've never gone back on: 100% whole-wheat atta, no maida, ever.

What you lose with maida

Milling wheat into maida removes most of its fibre, along with much of the iron, B-vitamins and slow-release energy. What's left behind digests fast and spikes quickly — the opposite of the steady, satisfying meal home food is meant to be.

"If we wouldn't make it that way at home, we won't make it that way for you."

Why whole wheat wins

Whole-wheat atta keeps the whole grain intact — fibre, minerals and a deeper, nuttier flavour. It's what gives a methi thepla its body and its keeping quality, and it's why our food sits lighter even when you go back for seconds.

  • More fibre and slow-release energy
  • Naturally retains iron and B-vitamins
  • A fuller, more home-style flavour

It's a small, stubborn choice — and it's the whole point of "Junk the Junk Food."

Tejal
Founder, Thepla House. Cooking ghar ka khana for Mumbai since 2018.
